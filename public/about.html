Jessi Ridgeway

Background

I was born in Cleveland, Ohio. When I was in 5th grade I attended a Cleveland Orchestra youth concert during a school field trip. I was amazed by the sound and remember recognizing the fourth movement of Beethoven's Fifth Symphony. The timpani player (Cloyd Duff) was fantastic to watch. I was completely mesmorized. When I was 11 years old, I started drum lessons and studied with some amazing teachers.

Education

I recieved my Bachelor Degree in Music in Percussion Performace from Stetson University in DeLand, FL and my Master Degre in Music in Percussion Performace from New England Conservatory in Boston, MA. I've been playing professionally in orchestras ever since!

Performing

Ever since I was 15years old, I have been playing drums for money. Each week I am given new music to learn for upcoming concert series. I spend a lot of time listening to and thinking about how I want to perform each piece. I make a plan to practice each day. Then every week I go onstage and perform some of the greatest pieces of music ever written by composers. It's a lot of fun to work with other artists and learn from their interpreations as well. In addition to performing live concerts, I have performed, engineered, and produced numerous recordings for various artists.

Other Professional Skills

Accounting & Bookkeeping

Besides performing professionally in orchetras, I also have other interests and skils. I teach private drum lessons to middle school, high school and college students. During COVID, when orchestras and concert halls were shut-down, I enrolled and was accepted into a university program to study accounting and computer science. I worked as bookkeeper for serveral years assisting two different accountants and working with hundreds of clients. Now, I now work remotely as a bookkeeper in my home office; this works well with my performing schedule.

Computer Programming & Web Development

During my studies at USF, I discovered that I really enjoyed creating web pages and learning new languages. Although most of my time then was spent creating Front-End website projects using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, I am now very interested in learning the Back-End side of development. I'm using the same work ethic to learn programming as I did with learning music. I create a plan for practicing and learning new languages and frameworks, create projects to practice, and hope to improve my knowledge and skills in order to make a career change and work professionally as a programmer.
